# Brownies recipe

## Ingredients

- 180 g dark chocolate, finely chopped (for melting)  
- 180 g butter  
- 4 tbsp sunflower oil or rapeseed (canola) oil  
- 6 medium eggs  
- 120 g white sugar  
- 120 g brown sugar  
- 140 g all-purpose (wheat) flour  
- 1 level tsp baking powder  
- 100 g unsweetened cocoa powder (baking cocoa)  
- 80 g dark chocolate, finely chopped (for folding in; reserve a few chunks for decoration)

## Instructions

1. Preheat the oven to **175 °C (conventional/top-and-bottom heat)**. Line a small baking tray or a rectangular baking pan (approx. **25 × 35 cm**) with parchment paper.  
2. Chop the chocolate that will be melted and the chocolate for folding in; set the latter aside (reserve a few chunks for decoration if desired).  
3. Melt the chopped chocolate and the butter together in a saucepan over a double boiler (hot water bath). Let cool slightly.  
4. Stir the oil into the melted chocolate–butter mixture.  
5. In a separate bowl, beat the eggs with the white and brown sugar until very frothy and voluminous.  
6. Mix the flour with the baking powder and cocoa powder.  
7. Add the melted chocolate–butter mixture and the dry ingredients to the egg–sugar cream and stir briefly but vigorously until combined.  
8. Fold in the chopped chocolate (keeping a few chunks for decoration). Pour the brownie batter into the prepared pan and smooth the top. Sprinkle the remaining chopped chocolate over the surface.

## Baking

- Bake the brownies for **about 20–28 minutes** — baking time depends on the thickness of the brownies and your oven.  
- Do **not** overbake: the batter should still be slightly soft in the center but not wobbling. The brownies will firm up a bit as they cool.
- Enjoy!
